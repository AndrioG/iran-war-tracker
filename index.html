Overall War Position Score

How to Read the Scores

All scores operate on a scale of −10 to +10. The Overall War Position Score is a weighted average of the six dimension scores below. A score of 0 represents a perfectly neutral or contested position. Positive values indicate advantage relative to each nation's stated war objectives; negative values indicate disadvantage.

+7 to +10 Dominant Advantage Near-total control of this dimension. Objectives being met decisively.
+4 to +7 Clear Advantage Meaningful lead. Opponent is under significant pressure in this area.
+1 to +4 Modest Advantage Slight edge. Outcomes leaning in this nation's favour but not decisive.
−1 to +1 Contested / Neutral No meaningful advantage either way. Situation is balanced or data is insufficient.
−4 to −1 Modest Disadvantage Slight setback. Opponent is gaining ground in this dimension.
−7 to −4 Clear Disadvantage Significant losses or setbacks. Objectives under serious threat.
−10 to −7 Critical Disadvantage Near-collapse in this dimension. Core objectives failing or already lost.

⚠️ Scores reflect automated analysis of recent news and are directional indicators, not definitive assessments. Dimensions showing zero indicate insufficient matched data for that cycle, not necessarily inactivity on the ground.
